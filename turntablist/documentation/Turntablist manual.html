Turntablist Manual

 

Welcome to Turntablist 1.1

Turntablist is an Audio Unit instrument that can load an audio sample and perform turntable style effects on it. You can scratch, change pitch, and even spin the audio up and down just like on a real turntable.

 

Coding by: Martin Robaszewski

Graphics by: Alterex

Audio Unit and 1.1+ updates by: Sophia Poirier

Power controls

OPEN FILE - use this to find an audio file to load up onto the "turntable"
POWER - switches the turntable power on or off
SPIN UP SPEED - how quickly the audio playback "spins up" when the turntable power turns on
SPIN DOWN SPEED - how quickly the audio playback "spins down" when the turntable power turns off
NOTE-POWER TRACK - enabling this will cause note-on and note-off messages to be mapped turntable power on and off for an interesting effect

Play controls

VOLUME - controls the overall volume of the audio output
MUTE - mutes the audio output
PLAY - push this on or off to start or stop the audio sample playback
NOTE MODE - toggles between "reset" and "resume" mode. When a new note begins, in resume mode, the audio sample will continue playing from where it last stopped, and in reset mode, the audio sample will start playing from the beginning.
DIRECTION - changes playback direction of the audio sample, regular or reverse
LOOP - if you enable this, the audio sample playback will continuously loop
KEY TRACK - switches key tracking on or off. When key tracking is on, the pitch and speed of the audio sample playback are transposed in relation to the pitch of the note. When key tracking is off, note pitches are ignored.
SCRATCH MODE - toggles between scrub mode and spin mode, which affects the behavior of the Scratch Amount parameter
MIDI LEARN - switches MIDI learn mode on or off

When MIDI learn is on, you can click on a parameter control to enable that parameter as the "learner." Then the next MIDI CC message that is received will be mapped to that parameter. From then on, you can control that parameter with MIDI CC messages of that type.

These MIDI CC → parameter assignments are saved with the plugin's settings.

HELP - push this to view the manual that you are reading right now

Pitch and Scratch controls

PITCH RANGE - this controls the range of pitch adjustment values that the Pitch Shift parameter offers
PITCH SHIFT - changes the audio playback pitch between +/- the Pitch Range value
SCRATCH SPEED - sets the speed of the scratching effect that the Scratch Amount parameter produces

In scrub mode, the range of Scratch Speed represents time from 0.5 to 5 seconds.

In spin mode, the range of Scratch Speed represents the maximum rate from 1 to 8 times the playback rate.
SCRATCH AMOUNT - This slider is what does the actual scratching.

In scrub mode, the entire range of the slider represents time (set by the Scratch Speed knob).

In spin mode, the top and bottom of the slider represent the maximum forward and backward speed (set by the Scratch Speed knob) while the center is 0.

If the Power and Play buttons are on and the Scratch Amount slider is in the center, scratching is disabled.

Using Turntablist

Getting results:

Load the plugin in an Audio Unit host and open an audio file.

Scratching:

Use the mouse to quickly move the Scratch Amount slider back and forth while the sample is playing. You can also turn the Power button off, and turn Play on and scratch without the sample having to play on its own. You can also use the pitch bend on your midi controller. Scratch mode 1 (off) is scrub, while scratch mode 2 (on) is the "spin" mode.

Spin down effect:

Turn NPTrack off, Power on and put Spin Down to around 50%. While Play button is on, turn the Power off.

Power effects:

Set the Spin Up and Spin Down speed knobs to diff values. Turn NPTrack on and turn Power on. Hit the Play button on and off and notice the sampling spinning up, playing and spinning down. This is a nice effect to use on beat loops.

 

A note about performance:

The scrub scratch mode will vary in response depending on your host. Some hosts send event updates to the plugin at a faster rate than others. Also the scratching is speed based (not position based) and will lose tracking after a while. How quickly it loses tracking depends on the host.

Version history:

1.1.0 - June 18-22, 2004

  • First Audio Unit release, by Sophia Poirier. Bioroid stopped development and released the source code, and Sophia decided to tinker with it.
  • now released under the terms of the GNU General Public License
  • fixed: numerous bugs in the audio file handling, which previously would not work in many cases (if files were located on non-boot volumes, if file paths were longer than 1024 bytes, if file names had Unicode characters, if file names were longer than 31 characters, etc.)

  • no longer requires that you install the libsndfile.dylib library
  • added "MIDI learn" feature
  • the manual is now built into the plugin
  • each control has pop-up help that appears when you hold you mouse over a control
